Thanks for testing! This page gives you a plain-English rundown of what's in the demo and what to try. You don't need any technical background — just tap through the things listed under "Give it a try" and let us know if anything feels off or confusing.
🧶 Explore the App Start here if you're new
Your projects
Browse the home screen. The app opens to your Active projects. Use the tabs at the top to switch between Active, Paused, Someday, and Done. Do the tabs feel natural?
Open a project. Tap any project card to open the detail view. You'll see the "Pick up here" note at the top, followed by the steps checklist and supplies list.
Complete a step. Inside a project, tap any step to mark it done. The step count on the card should update when you go back.
Mark a supply acquired. In the Supplies section, tap any supply to toggle it as acquired. Notice the shortfall warning disappears when you do.
Edit the "Pick up here" note. Inside a project, tap the note text to edit it inline. This is the most important field — does it feel easy to update?
Adding and managing projects
Create a new project. Tap the + button in the top right. Give it a title, a "pick up here" note, and pick a cover color. Tap Create — it should appear on the home screen immediately.
Change a project's status. From the home screen, tap the ⋯ menu on any project card. You can move it to Paused, Someday, or Done without opening the project.
Add a step. Open a project and tap the + next to Steps. Type a name and press Enter or tap Add. Does it appear in the right order?
Add a supply. Open a project and tap the + next to Supplies. Fill in the item name, quantity needed, quantity on hand, and unit. The shortfall (how many more you need) calculates automatically.
Step indicators
See the overdue warning. The Ceramic Mugs project has a step with a past due date — its card should show a red "⚠ overdue" indicator. Does it stand out clearly?
See a "due soon" indicator. The Autumn Throw Blanket has a step due in July — its card shows the upcoming step with a countdown. Does the label make sense?
See "all steps complete." Mark all steps done on any project and go back to the home screen. The card should now show a green "✓ All steps complete" indicator.
Free tier and upgrade
Check the project limit bar. The home screen shows a progress bar for your 5-project free tier limit. It should show 3 of 5 at the start with the seed projects.
See the upgrade prompt. Open any project and scroll to the bottom — the Progress Photos section shows an upgrade prompt for paid users. Does the message feel clear and not pushy?
Hit the project limit. Add projects until you reach 5, then try adding a 6th. The new project form should warn you rather than silently failing.
General notes
Data resets on refresh. This is expected — the demo has no real database. Anything you do (new projects, checked steps, edited notes) is temporary and clears when you refresh.
Light and dark mode. Tap the sun/moon icon in the top right to switch. The app follows your system preference by default.

Why it matters The app now uses the official color palette: sage green for primary actions (buttons, checkboxes, progress bars) and amethyst purple for highlights and accents like the "Pick up here" border and the wordmark period.

Why it matters The dark mode background is now the spec-correct deep charcoal (#1A1D23) rather than an earlier warm brown version that didn't match the brand sheet.

"Creativity tracker" tagline moved below the wordmark
Why it matters The tagline was sitting above the app name, which read backwards. It now sits underneath noodlr. in the header, the way a subheading should.

Font switched to Inter
Why it matters Per the branding spec, the app now uses Inter for all UI text. It's clean, high-contrast, and works well at small sizes on mobile.
Give it a try Read through a project card and the detail view. Does the text feel easy to read on your phone screen?
Step progress bar added to project detail view
Why it matters A thin progress bar now appears at the top of the detail view showing how many steps are done. It turns from sage green to olive when all steps are complete.
Give it a try Open the Autumn Throw Blanket — it has 2 of 5 steps done. Check the progress bar. Then mark all remaining steps complete and see if it fills and changes color.
Earlier What the app does 7 features
Home screen with project columns
Why it matters Projects are grouped into four columns — Active, Paused, Someday, and Done — so you always know what you're working on and what's waiting.
Give it a try Use the tabs at the top to switch columns. Move a project from Active to Paused using the ⋯ menu and confirm it disappears from Active and appears in Paused.
"Pick up here" note on every project
Why it matters This is the most important field in the app. It's a freeform note for where you left off — visible on the card and at the top of the detail view, always easy to edit.
Give it a try Open a project, tap the note, edit it, and save. Go back to the home screen — the updated note should appear on the card.
Step checklist with due dates and smart indicators
Why it matters Each project has an ordered step list. The home screen card shows one indicator — whichever is most urgent: overdue, due soon, next up, or all done.
Give it a try Open the Lino Print project and tap through a few steps to complete them. Check how the indicator on the card changes as you progress.
Supplies list with quantity tracking
Why it matters Each supply shows how much you need vs. how much you have, with a shortfall warning if you're short. Tap to mark it acquired.
Give it a try Open the Ceramic Mugs project — speckled glaze shows a shortfall. Tap it to mark it acquired and confirm the warning disappears.
Create new project
Why it matters Tap + to add a project with a title, pick-up-here note, description, deadline, status, and cover color. It appears on the home screen immediately.
Give it a try Tap + and create a project for something you're actually working on. Does the form ask for the right things? Is anything missing or confusing?
Free tier project cap
Why it matters The free version allows up to 5 projects total across all statuses. A progress bar on the home screen shows how many slots are used.
Give it a try Add projects until you're at the limit, then try adding one more. Does the warning feel clear and fair — not punitive?
Light and dark mode
Why it matters The app respects your phone's system appearance setting and can be toggled manually with the sun/moon icon in the header.
Give it a try Toggle between light and dark mode. Does everything stay readable in both? Does the dark mode feel comfortable or too heavy?
Notes Good to know
Data resets when you refresh
Known limitation This is expected behavior in the demo. Everything — new projects, completed steps, edited notes — is temporary. Refreshing the page wipes everything back to the three seed projects. The real version will save to a database per user account.
Cover photos aren't uploaded yet
Coming soon Right now projects use a color swatch instead of a real photo. Actual photo upload is planned — the color picker is a placeholder so cards don't look blank.
Progress photo gallery is upgrade-only
By design The progress photo section in each project shows an upgrade prompt rather than a working gallery. That's intentional — photo galleries are a paid feature. The prompt is placeholder UI for now; the actual payment flow isn't built yet.
