June 1 · 6:08 PM Noodle timer, microcopy overhaul, and bug fixes 6 changes
🍜 Noodle — focus session timer
Why it matters The biggest feature this session. Noodle is a gentle focus timer built specifically for neurodivergent users — people who struggle with task initiation, overwhelm, and "where do I even start?" It's accessible from every project card (🍜 button) and from the project detail view.
How it works Three phases: a warm-up ritual with pre-checked encouragement boxes and a single "up next" step; a timer with a soft progress ring and rotating nudge messages; and a warm finish screen with the session logged to the project timeline automatically. Duration options: 5, 10, 20 min, or free run.
Give it a try Tap 🍜 on any project card. Check off the warm-up boxes, pick a time, and start. Try stopping early with "I'm done for now" — it should feel kind, not punishing. Then check the project's Timeline to see the session logged.
Rotating daily greeting
Why it matters The very first thing you see when you open the app is now a warm, rotating phrase — different for morning, afternoon, and evening. 30 phrases across the three pools, mostly encouraging with a few silly ones mixed in ("Good morning — the glue gun is warmed up.").
Give it a try Refresh a few times to see different phrases. The greeting sits above the Due Today card — it's the first thing you land on.
Full microcopy overhaul — warmer tone throughout
Why it matters Every empty state, button label, placeholder, and confirmation in the app has been rewritten to match noodlr's new direction: friendly, encouraging, and neurodivergent-aware. The app now speaks like a supportive friend, not a productivity tool. Examples: "No steps yet. Break it down — even one tiny thing counts ✨" / "Where did you leave off? Tap to drop a note — future you will be so grateful." / "You've got this. noodlr."
Strategic pivot — neurodivergent-first positioning
Why it matters noodlr is now explicitly designed for ADHDers and neurodivergent creators — people with many half-finished projects who need help with task initiation, overwhelm, and "what next?" rather than another rigid productivity system. This shapes all future feature decisions.
Create project bug fix
What was wrong Creating a new project silently failed in sandboxed/iframe preview environments. A localStorage call in the greeting function was throwing a security error and killing the entire init block — so renderHome() never ran after project creation. Wrapped in try/catch; now fails gracefully.
Noodle sessions logged to Timeline
Why it matters When a Noodle session ends (timer complete or stopped early), the session is automatically logged as a journal entry on the project's Timeline — "🍜 Noodle session — 12 mins". Builds a record of creative momentum over time.

Your projects
Browse the home screen. The app opens to your Active projects. Use the tabs at the top to switch between Active, Paused, Someday, and Done. Do the tabs feel natural?
Open a project. Tap any project card to open the detail view. You'll see the "Pick up here" note at the top, followed by the steps checklist and supplies list.
Complete a step. Inside a project, tap any step to mark it done. The step count on the card should update when you go back.
Mark a supply acquired. In the Supplies section, tap any supply to toggle it as acquired. Notice the shortfall warning disappears when you do.
Edit the "Pick up here" note. Inside a project, tap the note text to edit it inline. This is the most important field — does it feel easy to update?
Adding and managing projects
Create a new project. Tap the + button in the top right. Give it a title, a "pick up here" note, and pick a cover color. Tap Create — it should appear on the home screen immediately.
Change a project's status. From the home screen, tap the ⋯ menu on any project card. You can move it to Paused, Someday, or Done without opening the project.
Add a step. Open a project and tap the + next to Steps. Type a name and press Enter or tap Add. Does it appear in the right order?
Add a supply. Open a project and tap the + next to Supplies. Fill in the item name, quantity needed, quantity on hand, and unit. The shortfall (how many more you need) calculates automatically.

General notes
Data resets on refresh. This is expected — the demo has no real database. Anything you do (new projects, checked steps, edited notes) is temporary and clears when you refresh.
Light and dark mode. Tap the sun/moon icon in the top right to switch. The app follows your system preference by default.

Notes Good to know
Data resets when you refresh
Known limitation This is expected behavior in the demo. Everything — new projects, completed steps, edited notes — is temporary. Refreshing the page wipes everything back to the three seed projects. The real version will save to a database per user account.
Cover photos aren't uploaded yet
Coming soon Right now projects use a color swatch instead of a real photo. Actual photo upload is planned — the color picker is a placeholder so cards don't look blank.
Progress photo gallery is upgrade-only
By design The progress photo section in each project shows an upgrade prompt rather than a working gallery. That's intentional — photo galleries are a paid feature. The prompt is placeholder UI for now; the actual payment flow isn't built yet.
